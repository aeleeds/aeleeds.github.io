Adam E. Leeds
About | Research | Curriculum Vitae
I am an anthropologist concerned with political rationalities and technologies of government. In particular, I am interested in the imagination and assembly of forms of political order that could be understood as liberalisms by means of the forms of knowledge called economics. My current project is a historical ethnography of the economists of Moscow in the Soviet Union and contemporary Russia, who figure as key agents in the elaboration of alternative liberalisms and socialisms in the modern political imaginary.

Currently I am a doctoral candidate in the Depart of Anthropology of the University of Pennsylvania, and a Predoctoral Fellow at the Center for the History of Political Economy at Duke University.
